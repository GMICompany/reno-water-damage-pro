Common Questions About Water Damage in Reno

How quickly can you respond to water damage emergencies in Reno?

We respond to water damage emergencies in Reno within 60 minutes or less, 24/7. Our trucks are stationed throughout Washoe County, including South Reno, Midtown, and Spanish Springs. Time matters with water damage because mold can start growing within 24-48 hours. The faster we extract water and start drying, the less damage occurs and the lower your restoration costs.

What does water damage restoration cost in Reno?

Water damage restoration in Reno typically costs between $1,800 and $6,500 depending on the extent of damage. Minor leaks affecting one room (like a bathroom or laundry room) start around $1,800-$2,500. Moderate damage affecting multiple rooms runs $3,000-$4,500. Severe flooding affecting multiple rooms or basement areas ranges from $4,000-$6,500. We provide free estimates and work directly with insurance companies to maximize your coverage.

Do you work with homeowners insurance for water damage claims?

Yes, we work directly with all major insurance companies including State Farm, Allstate, USAA, Farmers, and Liberty Mutual. We document everything with photos and moisture readings, provide detailed estimates in the format adjusters expect, and communicate directly with your insurance company throughout the process. Most homeowners policies cover sudden water damage like burst pipes and appliance failures. We'll help you understand what's covered.

What areas of Reno do you serve?

We serve all of Reno and surrounding Washoe County including South Reno, Midtown, Old Southwest, Spanish Springs, Sparks, Sun Valley, Cold Springs, and Lemmon Valley. We also respond to emergencies in nearby communities like Incline Village, Carson City, and Fernley when needed. Our central location near S Virginia Street allows us to reach most of Reno within 30-45 minutes.

How long does water damage restoration take?

Most residential water damage jobs take 3-7 days from start to finish. Day 1 involves water extraction and setting up drying equipment (air movers and dehumidifiers). Days 2-5 are active drying with daily moisture checks. Once everything is completely dry, we remove equipment and begin any necessary repairs like drywall replacement or flooring. Severe flood damage can take 2-3 weeks if major reconstruction is needed.

What should I do immediately after discovering water damage?

First, stop the water source if possible (turn off main water valve, shut off appliance, etc.). Second, call us at (775) 555-0142 for immediate response. Third, move valuable items to dry areas if safe to do so. Do NOT use regular household vacuums on standing water (electrical hazard). Avoid walking through standing water. Take photos for insurance documentation. The most important thing is to call professionals quickly because every hour of delay increases damage and mold risk.
