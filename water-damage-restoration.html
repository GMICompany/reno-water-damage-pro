What is Water Damage Restoration?

Water damage restoration is the complete process of returning your property to its pre-damage condition after water intrusion. This includes emergency water extraction, structural drying, dehumidification, cleaning, sanitization, and repairs. In Reno, water damage most commonly occurs from burst pipes during winter freezes, failed water heaters, leaking roofs during rare heavy rains, and appliance malfunctions.

The restoration process must happen quickly because water damage gets worse with time. Within the first 24 hours, drywall begins absorbing water, wood floors start warping, and mold spores begin activating. After 48 hours, mold and bacteria growth accelerates, and structural damage becomes more severe. That's why we guarantee 60-minute emergency response throughout Reno and Washoe County.

Common Causes of Water Damage in Reno Homes

Burst Pipes

Reno's winter temperatures regularly drop below freezing, especially in areas like South Reno and Spanish Springs. When pipes freeze and burst, they can release hundreds of gallons of water into your home. This is the most common cause of major water damage we see, particularly in January and February. Burst pipes often happen in exterior walls, crawl spaces, and attics where insulation is inadequate.

Water Heater Failures

Water heaters typically last 8-12 years. When they fail, they often release 40-80 gallons of water all at once. This usually happens in garages or utility rooms, and the water spreads quickly to adjacent rooms. We respond to water heater failures almost weekly in Reno, and most homeowners had no warning signs before the tank failed.

Appliance Leaks

Washing machines, dishwashers, and refrigerators with ice makers all have water supply lines that can fail. A washing machine supply line failure can release water at full pressure until someone shuts off the valve. Dishwasher leaks often go unnoticed for days or weeks, causing hidden damage under cabinets and flooring.

Roof Leaks

While Reno only gets about 7 inches of rain per year, when storms do hit, they can be intense. Monsoon-style thunderstorms in summer and occasional heavy winter storms expose weak spots in roofing. Old or damaged shingles, failed flashing around chimneys, and ice dams can all allow water into attics and ceilings.

Slab Leaks

Many Reno homes built on concrete slabs have water lines running beneath the foundation. When these pipes corrode or shift, they leak water under the slab, which eventually appears as wet spots on floors, unexplained water bills, or foundation cracks. Slab leaks require specialized detection equipment and often extensive repairs.

After extraction, we set up commercial air movers and dehumidifiers to dry the structure. Air movers create airflow across wet surfaces to accelerate evaporation. This phase typically takes 3-5 days for most residential jobs.

Step 4: Monitoring and Adjustment (Daily)

Our technicians visit daily to take moisture readings with calibrated meters. We check wall cavities, subfloors, and other hidden areas to ensure drying is progressing. Once the structure is dry, we clean and sanitize all affected areas. We use EPA-approved antimicrobial treatments to prevent mold growth. Carpets receive professional cleaning and deodorization. Water Damage Categories: What They Mean for Your Restoration

Category 1: Clean Water

Clean water comes from broken water supply lines, melting ice or snow, or rainwater that hasn't contacted contaminants. This is the least harmful type of water damage. However, if not addressed within 48 hours, clean water can degrade to Category 2 as it absorbs contaminants from building materials.

Category 2: Gray Water

Gray water contains some contaminants and comes from sources like washing machine discharge, dishwasher overflow, or toilet overflow with urine only. This water requires additional sanitization during restoration. Materials exposed to gray water may need to be disposed of rather than dried, depending on absorption levels.

Category 3: Black Water

Black water is highly contaminated and comes from sewage backups, flooding from rivers or streams, or toilet overflow with feces. This requires specialized handling with full protective equipment. Most porous materials exposed to black water must be removed and disposed of. Category 3 restoration costs significantly more due to safety requirements and disposal needs.

Most homeowners insurance policies cover sudden and accidental water damage. This includes burst pipes, appliance failures, sudden water heater leaks, and roof leaks from storm damage. Insurance typically does NOT cover damage from lack of maintenance, gradual leaks that developed over time, or flooding from external sources like the Truckee River (that requires separate flood insurance).

Preventing Water Damage in Your Reno Home

While we're always here to help when water damage occurs, prevention is the best strategy. Here are specific recommendations for Reno homeowners:

  • Winterize pipes: Insulate pipes in exterior walls, attics, and crawl spaces before winter. Keep cabinet doors open during freezes to allow warm air to reach pipes. Let faucets drip when temperatures drop below 25°F.
  • Replace water heaters proactively: If your water heater is over 10 years old, replace it before it fails. Consider upgrading to a tankless system to eliminate flood risk.
  • Replace appliance hoses: Washing machine and dishwasher hoses should be replaced every 5 years. Upgrade to braided stainless steel hoses, which are far more durable than rubber.
  • Install water alarms: Place battery-powered water alarms near water heaters, under sinks, behind toilets, and near washing machines. They're $15 each and can alert you to leaks before major damage occurs.
  • Inspect your roof annually: Even though Reno is dry, inspect your roof every year for damaged shingles, failed flashing, and other weak points. Fix small problems before they become big ones.
  • Know where your main water shutoff is: Every household member should know where the main water shutoff valve is located and how to turn it off in an emergency.

What to Do When You Discover Water Damage

If you discover water damage in your Reno home, follow these steps:

  1. Stop the water source if possible. Turn off the main water valve if it's a plumbing failure. Shut off the specific appliance if you can identify it. If it's a roof leak, place buckets to catch drips and minimize spreading.
  2. Call us immediately at (775) 555-0142. Don't wait to see if it gets worse. The faster we start, the less damage occurs and the lower your costs.
  3. Move valuables to dry areas. If it's safe to do so, move furniture, electronics, and important documents to unaffected rooms. Don't put yourself at risk.
  4. Take photos for insurance. Document the damage with your phone before we arrive. This helps with insurance claims.
  5. Don't use household vacuums. Regular vacuums aren't designed for water and create electrocution risks. Don't use them on standing water.
  6. Avoid walking through contaminated water. If the water looks or smells questionable, stay out of it until we arrive with proper protective equipment.
